Ideal VC Brain UI Flow
The UI should begin with an investor’s thesis and end with a human-approved investment decision, backed by evidence. The system assists; it never silently decides or sends money.
Start: investor setup and dashboard
The first screen is a simple investor dashboard:
Configure or select a fund thesis: sectors, geography, stage, check size, risk appetite, must-haves, and deal breakers.
See a ranked decision queue, incoming applications, recently discovered founders, candidates close to the 24-hour deadline, and candidates needing review.
Start either path:Inbound: a founder submits company name, deck, and optional website, GitHub, demo, and work samples.
Outbound: an investor searches or monitors sources such as GitHub, hackathons, launch posts, papers, and accelerators.

Candidate workspace: the main working screen
Every inbound or outbound candidate lands in the same workspace.
The investor sees:
Candidate and company profile, source timeline, founder history, and linked evidence.
A clear “what the system knows / does not know” panel.
Three separate scores: Founder, Market, and Idea vs. Market - never one blended score.
Claim-level trust: source, quote/snippet, freshness, corroboration, contradictions, and confidence.
A 24-hour decision timer.
The investor can intervene at any point:
Approve or reject identity matches and duplicates.
Add a deck, website, GitHub repo, reference, note, or source manually.
Mark a claim as disputed, verified, irrelevant, or requiring follow-up.
Ask the system to refresh research, run a targeted search, or request information from the founder.
Change the fund thesis or switch the candidate to another thesis, then re-run scoring.
Pause, cancel, or resume background research.
Override a recommendation, but only with a written rationale.
Important: edits should create a new version or audit event. The UI must preserve what the system originally concluded and why; a user should not be able to silently rewrite evidence or scores.
Diligence and memo
Once the investor is satisfied with the evidence, they open a decision-ready memo:
Company snapshot and investment hypothesis.
Founder/team evidence.
Market, competition, SWOT, product, traction, and risks.
Explicit evidence citations beside material claims.
Gaps and the next best diligence questions.
Bull case, bear case, kill criteria, and recommendation conditions.
The investor can regenerate individual sections after adding evidence, but the old memo remains available as a version.
End: human decision and handoff
The final screen asks for one intentional action:
Invest: e.g. “Recommend $100K, subject to reference verification.”
Watchlist: keep monitoring signals and specify what would change the decision.
Request information: send a human-approved follow-up request.
Pass: record an evidence-based rationale.
The output is not merely a score. It is a persistent candidate record containing:
decision and rationale;
full memo;
evidence and contradictions;
score history;
founder profile that improves with future signals;
source-channel outcome data for future sourcing.
After “Invest,” the hackathon scope ends with a handoff to fund operations. Portfolio management, contracts, and money transfer remain outside this product.
Minimum user input vs. final output
Stage	User provides	User receives
Fund setup	Investment thesis	Filter and scoring lens
Inbound	Company name + deck; optional links	Parsed candidate and initial evidence
Outbound	Search/thesis or monitored sources	Ranked founder candidates
Review	Corrections, extra documents, follow-up requests	Refreshed scores and trust assessment
Decision	Final human rationale	Investment memo and auditable outcome

This is the UI flow the current backend should eventually support. The key experience is a calm investor workspace: the AI gathers and explains; the human reviews, challenges, modifies, and makes the final call.
